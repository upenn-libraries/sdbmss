Seller 1

Enter name of auction house, dealer, or private seller. Use if the transaction represents a sale or attempt at a sale, or an exchange between one individual or institution to another.
e.g.:    Sotheby's
  Sotheby's NYC
  Sotheby's Tel Aviv
  American Art

If an individual dealer or seller's firm is identified by his name, give the legal name of the firm. If a private individual seller, give last name first. If a donation, add "(donation)" after individual's name.
e.g.:    Leo S. Olschki
  Leon Gruel
  Lewis, John Frederick (donation)

SELLER1 is, first, the auction house if known. If it is not given, then it is the expert in charge or the director of the sale, usually a seller or dealer. If that person is not identified, then the publisher of the catalogue is given.

When it is difficult to identify the seller, especially in early sales and auction catalogues, follow the standards set forth in Répertoire des catalogues de ventes de livres imprimés vols. 1-5 (Jeanne Blogie; Brussels, 1982-1996; p. i).

Note: Render all accented letters as the equivalent without diacritical marks. e.g., à / á / â ⇒ a, æ ⇒ ae, ä ⇒ ae, ç ⇒ c, è / é / ê ⇒ e, ì / í / î ⇒ i, œ ⇒ oe, ö ⇒ oe, ü ⇒ ue, etc.
