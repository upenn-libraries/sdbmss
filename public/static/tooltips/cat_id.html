Cat ID

Enter the title or identifying statement of a catalogue. If an author is identified, add name (first initial, then last name) at end of title. Put in parentheses.

In case of sale catalogue, enter title, number and title (or just number if no title given), collection, or other statement of identification of the catalogue. Shorten long titles by excluding subtitles and/or least relevant terms and replacing them with an ellipsis (...). For instance, we can abbreviate Manuscripts, chiefly upon vellum, in various languages of Europe and the East, formed by M. Guglielmo Libri, the eminent collector, who is obliged to leave London in consequence of ill health, and for that reason to dispose of his literary treasures into a briefer version as shown below.
e.g.:    Manuscripts, chiefly upon vellum, in various languages of Europe and the East, formed by M. Guglielmo Libri ...
e.g.: no. 5094: Indian and Islamic works of art. (for Christie's April 20, 2007 sale)
